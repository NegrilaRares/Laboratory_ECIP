The population was modeled using the logistic equation

x_(t+1)=r*x_t*(1-x_t)

where x_t is the population at step t and r is the growth factor. 

In this case, the growth factor was r = 0.9 and the initial population was 0.5.

After several iterations, the population gradually decreases. Starting from 0.5, the value quickly drops and continues to become smaller at each step, eventually approaching zero.

This happens because the growth factor is less than 1, meaning the reproduction rate is not strong enough to sustain the population. As a result, the population continuously declines.

Therefore, for r = 0.9, the model shows that the population will eventually go extinct.
